50 Most Common Grammar Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Usage December 2024 20 min read

Grammar mistakes can undermine your credibility and clarity in both written and spoken English. Whether you're a student, professional, or English learner, avoiding these common errors will significantly improve your communication skills. This comprehensive guide covers the 50 most frequent grammar mistakes with clear explanations and corrections.

Subject-Verb Agreement Errors

1. Singular/Plural Subject Confusion

❌ Wrong: The group of students are coming.
✅ Correct: The group of students is coming.

Rule: Focus on the main subject, not the prepositional phrase.

2. Compound Subjects with "And"

❌ Wrong: John and Mary is coming to the party.
✅ Correct: John and Mary are coming to the party.

Rule: Compound subjects joined by "and" take plural verbs.

3. Either/Or and Neither/Nor

❌ Wrong: Either the teacher or the students is responsible.
✅ Correct: Either the teacher or the students are responsible.

Rule: The verb agrees with the subject closest to it.

4. Collective Nouns

❌ Wrong: The team are playing well.
✅ Correct: The team is playing well.

Rule: Collective nouns usually take singular verbs when acting as a unit.

5. Indefinite Pronouns

❌ Wrong: Everyone have their own opinion.
✅ Correct: Everyone has their own opinion.

Rule: Most indefinite pronouns (everyone, someone, nobody) are singular.

Pronoun Usage Errors

6. Pronoun Case Errors

❌ Wrong: Between you and I, this is wrong.
✅ Correct: Between you and me, this is wrong.

Rule: Use object pronouns after prepositions.

7. Who vs. Whom

❌ Wrong: Who did you give the book to?
✅ Correct: Whom did you give the book to?

Rule: Use "whom" when it's the object of a verb or preposition.

8. Reflexive Pronoun Misuse

❌ Wrong: John and myself went to the store.
✅ Correct: John and I went to the store.

Rule: Don't use reflexive pronouns as subjects.

9. Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement

❌ Wrong: Each student must bring their book.
✅ Correct: Each student must bring his or her book.

Rule: Pronouns must agree with their antecedents in number and gender.

10. Vague Pronoun Reference

❌ Wrong: John told Mike that he was wrong.
✅ Correct: John told Mike that Mike was wrong.

Rule: Make pronoun references clear and unambiguous.

Tense and Verb Form Errors

11. Inconsistent Tense

❌ Wrong: I went to the store and buy some milk.
✅ Correct: I went to the store and bought some milk.

Rule: Maintain consistent tense throughout your writing.

12. Present Perfect vs. Simple Past

❌ Wrong: I have seen him yesterday.
✅ Correct: I saw him yesterday.

Rule: Use simple past with specific time references.

13. Irregular Verb Forms

❌ Wrong: I have wrote the letter.
✅ Correct: I have written the letter.

Rule: Learn irregular past participle forms.

14. Conditional Sentences

❌ Wrong: If I would have known, I would have come.
✅ Correct: If I had known, I would have come.

Rule: Don't use "would" in the if-clause of conditional sentences.

15. Subjunctive Mood

❌ Wrong: I wish I was taller.
✅ Correct: I wish I were taller.

Rule: Use subjunctive "were" for hypothetical situations.

Article Usage Errors

16. A vs. An

❌ Wrong: I need a umbrella.
✅ Correct: I need an umbrella.

Rule: Use "an" before vowel sounds, "a" before consonant sounds.

17. Missing Articles

❌ Wrong: I went to store.
✅ Correct: I went to the store.

Rule: Use articles with countable singular nouns.

18. Unnecessary Articles

❌ Wrong: I love the music.
✅ Correct: I love music.

Rule: Don't use articles with general concepts or abstract nouns.

19. Articles with Proper Nouns

❌ Wrong: I live in the New York.
✅ Correct: I live in New York.

Rule: Most proper nouns don't take articles.

20. Articles with Superlatives

❌ Wrong: She is best student in class.
✅ Correct: She is the best student in the class.

Rule: Always use "the" with superlatives.

Preposition Errors

21. In vs. On vs. At (Time)

❌ Wrong: I'll see you on Monday morning.
✅ Correct: I'll see you on Monday morning. (This is actually correct!)
❌ Wrong: I was born on 1990.
✅ Correct: I was born in 1990.

Rule: Use "in" for years/months, "on" for days/dates, "at" for specific times.

22. In vs. On vs. At (Place)

❌ Wrong: I live on New York.
✅ Correct: I live in New York.

Rule: Use "in" for cities/countries, "on" for streets, "at" for specific addresses.

23. Different From vs. Different Than

❌ Wrong: This is different than that.
✅ Correct: This is different from that.

Rule: Use "different from" in formal writing.

24. Interested In vs. Interested On

❌ Wrong: I'm interested on learning Spanish.
✅ Correct: I'm interested in learning Spanish.

Rule: Use "interested in" for topics or activities.

25. Depend On vs. Depend Of

❌ Wrong: It depends of the weather.
✅ Correct: It depends on the weather.

Rule: Always use "depend on" or "depends on."

Punctuation Mistakes

26. Comma Splices

❌ Wrong: I went to the store, I bought some milk.
✅ Correct: I went to the store, and I bought some milk.

Rule: Don't join independent clauses with just a comma.

27. Apostrophe Misuse

❌ Wrong: The dog wagged it's tail.
✅ Correct: The dog wagged its tail.

Rule: "Its" is possessive; "it's" means "it is."

28. Missing Commas in Series

❌ Wrong: I bought apples oranges and bananas.
✅ Correct: I bought apples, oranges, and bananas.

Rule: Use commas to separate items in a series.

29. Quotation Mark Placement

❌ Wrong: She said "Hello".
✅ Correct: She said "Hello."

Rule: Periods and commas go inside quotation marks.

30. Semicolon Misuse

❌ Wrong: I like pizza; and pasta.
✅ Correct: I like pizza and pasta.

Rule: Don't use semicolons with coordinating conjunctions.

Word Choice and Usage Errors

31. Your vs. You're

❌ Wrong: Your going to love this.
✅ Correct: You're going to love this.

Rule: "Your" is possessive; "you're" means "you are."

32. There vs. Their vs. They're

❌ Wrong: Their going to there house.
✅ Correct: They're going to their house.

Rule: "There" = place, "their" = possessive, "they're" = "they are."

33. Affect vs. Effect

❌ Wrong: The rain will effect our plans.
✅ Correct: The rain will affect our plans.

Rule: "Affect" is a verb; "effect" is usually a noun.

34. Accept vs. Except

❌ Wrong: I like all colors accept red.
✅ Correct: I like all colors except red.

Rule: "Accept" means to receive; "except" means excluding.

35. Lose vs. Loose

❌ Wrong: Don't loose your keys.
✅ Correct: Don't lose your keys.

Rule: "Lose" is a verb; "loose" is an adjective.

Sentence Structure Errors

36. Run-on Sentences

❌ Wrong: I went to the store I bought milk I came home.
✅ Correct: I went to the store, bought milk, and came home.

Rule: Break long sentences into shorter, clearer ones.

37. Sentence Fragments

❌ Wrong: Because I was tired.
✅ Correct: I went to bed early because I was tired.

Rule: Every sentence needs a subject and predicate.

38. Dangling Modifiers

❌ Wrong: Walking to school, the rain started.
✅ Correct: Walking to school, I got caught in the rain.

Rule: Make sure modifiers clearly relate to the right noun.

39. Misplaced Modifiers

❌ Wrong: I saw a dog walking down the street with three legs.
✅ Correct: I saw a three-legged dog walking down the street.

Rule: Place modifiers close to what they modify.

40. Parallel Structure

❌ Wrong: I like swimming, running, and to bike.
✅ Correct: I like swimming, running, and biking.

Rule: Use parallel structure in lists and series.

Additional Common Mistakes (41-50)

41. Could of → Could have

❌ I could of done better. ✅ I could have done better.

42. Alot → A lot

❌ I have alot of work. ✅ I have a lot of work.

43. Irregardless → Regardless

❌ Irregardless of the cost. ✅ Regardless of the cost.

44. Less vs. Fewer

❌ Less people came. ✅ Fewer people came.

45. Good vs. Well

❌ I did good on the test. ✅ I did well on the test.

46. Lay vs. Lie

❌ I'm going to lay down. ✅ I'm going to lie down.

47. Than vs. Then

❌ First we eat, than we leave. ✅ First we eat, then we leave.

48. Who's vs. Whose

❌ Whose coming to dinner? ✅ Who's coming to dinner?

49. Complement vs. Compliment

❌ That shirt complements your eyes. ✅ That shirt complements your eyes. (This is correct!)

50. Everyday vs. Every day

❌ I exercise everyday. ✅ I exercise every day.

How to Avoid These Mistakes

1. Read Aloud

Reading your writing aloud helps catch errors your eyes might miss.

2. Use Grammar Tools

Tools like Grammarly can help identify common mistakes, but don't rely on them completely.

3. Practice Regularly

Regular practice with grammar exercises helps internalize correct patterns.

4. Learn the Rules

Understanding the underlying grammar rules helps you apply them correctly.

5. Get Feedback

Have others review your writing to catch mistakes you might overlook.

6. Keep a Mistake Log

Track your common errors to focus your improvement efforts.

Conclusion

Avoiding these 50 common grammar mistakes will significantly improve your English writing and speaking. Remember that mastering grammar is a gradual process that requires consistent practice and attention to detail. Focus on one category at a time, and gradually incorporate correct usage into your daily communication.
